The Intelligence Pipeline: From Data to Decision

Intelligence emerges from physics + data + anomaly detection

1
SENSORS & TELEMETRY
High-frequency data collection (pressure, temperature, vibration, shaft speed) from the physical engine.
2
DATA INGESTION
Real-time transmission and processing of vast telemetry streams.
3
DIGITAL TWIN SIMULATION
Physics-based models simulate the engine's expected performance, providing a baseline for comparison. Simulates thermodynamic cycles, stress distributions, vibration modes, and thermal loads in real time.
4
ANOMALY DETECTION
Machine Learning models identify subtle deviations that indicate potential faults. Example: a 15°C EGT deviation from the twin’s predicted curve indicates turbine wear.
5
MAINTENANCE DECISIONS
Actionable insights (health score, risk projection) are generated to schedule maintenance and prevent AOG events.
SYSTEM CAPABILITIES:
Detects sensor failures (drift, noise, bias)
Flags abnormal vibration signatures
Identifies thermal-pattern deviations
Estimates remaining useful life (RUL)
