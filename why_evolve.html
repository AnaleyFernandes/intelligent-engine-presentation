The Imperative for Evolution: Pressures on Aviation

ENVIRONMENTAL PRESSURE
Fuel burn, CO₂ emissions, and noise regulations are tightening globally, demanding radical efficiency gains. Aviation CO₂ emissions are projected to triple by 2050 without intervention (ICAO forecast).
SAFETY PRESSURE
Recent incidents demonstrate the need for real-time failure prediction and anomaly detection systems. Despite being the safest era in aviation, 2024-2025 still saw multiple catastrophic incidents, proving safety must continue to evolve.
RELIABILITY & COST
Unplanned engine removals (AOG) cost airlines hundreds of thousands of dollars per day, about £100,000–£300,000 per incident (IATA).
TECHNOLOGICAL LIMIT
Gas turbines are mature; incremental mechanical improvements are reaching diminishing returns.
