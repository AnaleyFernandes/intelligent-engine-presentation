Defining the Intelligent Engine

An engine that analyses itself, adapts to conditions, and predicts failures before they occur.

1
PHYSICAL ENGINE + DIGITAL TWIN
A real-time, physics-based virtual replica of the engine that operates in parallel with the physical hardware.
2
SELF-DIAGNOSING
Constant, automated health monitoring across thousands of data points (pressure, temperature, vibration, speed). Identifies root causes using physics + sensor data + historical behaviour.
3
PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE
Shifting from time-based or reactive maintenance to condition-based care, ensuring parts are replaced based on estimated remaining useful life (RUL). Forecast degradation patterns before they reach unsafe limits.
4
CONTINUOUS OPTIMIZATION
Real-time performance adjustments based on environmental factors and component degradation to maintain peak efficiency. Adjusts thrust, fuel scheduling, and cooling based on real-time conditions.
